**🧑 User:** Yes — how's the ride, and what are the main downsides?

**🤖 AutoSage:** On ride quality the C3 does well for its class. It uses Citroën's hydraulic-cushion dampers, so it soaks up bumps better than most small cars — though a short wheelbase means big, sharp intrusions still come through, and wind and road noise are more present than you'd like.

The main downsides from the review:
- **Materials & cabin feel** — lots of hard, plain plastic; the cost-cutting is visible.
- **Drivetrain** — the mild-hybrid 1.2 turbo is economical but not the smoothest; the dual-clutch gearbox can hesitate in stop-go traffic, and the always-on regenerative braking makes progress feel less smooth.
- **Basic safety/equipment** — it covers the essentials but no adaptive cruise control, for example.

Net: it trades polish for a low price. If comfort and cost matter more to you than a premium feel, that trade works out well.

> *Sources: Citroën C3 (2026)*
